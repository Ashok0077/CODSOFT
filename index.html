Swami Vivekananda

Name: Swami Vivekananda

Date of Birth: January 12, 1863

Father's Name: Vishwanath Datta

Mother's Name: Bhuvaneshwari Devi

Place of Birth: Kolkata, India

Swami Vivekananda was an Indian monk, a key figure in the introduction of Indian philosophies of Vedanta and Yoga to the Western world.

Achievements

Swami Vivekananda is best known for his inspiring speech at the Parliament of the World's Religions in Chicago in 1893. He introduced Hinduism to the Western audience and gained worldwide recognition for his wisdom and charisma.

Quotes

"Arise, awake and stop not until the goal is reached."
"In a conflict between the heart and the brain, follow your heart."

Hobbies

  • Meditation
  • Reading scriptures
  • Travelling

Awards

  • Bharat Ratna - India's highest civilian award (1999)
  • Order of the Star of India (1897)
  • Order of St. Vladimir (1896)
